Bank Marketing Case Study

The dataset is related to direct marketing campaigns of a banking institution, focusing on predicting whether a client will subscribe to a term deposit using Machine Learning algorithms.

About the Dataset

The data includes information about clients, contact details, and outcomes of marketing campaigns. Key variables cover age, job, marital status, education, credit default, balance, housing loan, personal loan, contact type, and more.

Exploratory Data Analysis

The analysis reveals insights such as the distribution of term deposit subscriptions, customer demographics, marital status influence, and the impact of variables like credit default, housing loan, and personal loan on subscription decisions.

Classification Models

Four classification models—Logistic Regression, KNN, Decision Tree, and Random Forest—were applied. Random Forest outperformed others with the highest AUC ROC score (82.99%) and accuracy (82.92%).

Conclusion

Random Forest proves to be the best classifier for the dataset, offering robust predictive capabilities for term deposit subscriptions in the banking marketing scenario.
